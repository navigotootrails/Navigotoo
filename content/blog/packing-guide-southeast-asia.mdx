## The Philosophy: One Bag, No Regrets

The single biggest mistake first-time Southeast Asia travellers make is overpacking. You will sweat through everything. Laundry is cheap and everywhere. There is no occasion in Southeast Asia for which a blazer is required.

Our rule: if you can't carry your bag comfortably for 20 minutes, it's too heavy.

## The Bag

We use a **40-litre carry-on compatible backpack** with a separate laptop sleeve. This keeps you off the checked baggage carousel, forces you to pack light, and makes budget airline travel far cheaper.

Recommended picks: Osprey Farpoint 40, Tortuga Setout 45 (just within most carry-on limits).

## Clothing

Pack for 4–5 days, not the full trip length. You will do laundry.

**Essentials:**
- 3 lightweight t-shirts (merino wool or synthetic — cotton is a sweat trap)
- 2 pairs of lightweight trousers (one for trekking, one smarter)
- 1 pair of shorts
- 5 pairs of underwear (merino or ExOfficio)
- 4 pairs of socks
- 1 lightweight down jacket (even in hot climates, buses and temples are cold)
- 1 rain jacket (non-negotiable)
- 1 pair of flip flops
- 1 pair of trail runners

**Leave behind:** Jeans (too heavy and slow to dry), formal shoes, more than one pair of shoes beyond flip flops.

## Toiletries

Buy most of these in-country. They're cheaper, the same quality, and you won't have liquids confiscated at security.

**Bring from home:**
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+ is hard to find outside cities)
- Deet-based insect repellent (especially for jungle areas)
- Your prescription medications
- A small first aid kit: blister plasters, ibuprofen, antihistamines, rehydration sachets

## Electronics

- Unlocked phone + buy a local SIM on arrival (much cheaper than roaming)
- Universal adapter
- Portable power bank (10,000mAh minimum)
- Headphones

## Documents

- Physical copies of passport data page, insurance, and accommodation confirmations — kept separately from the originals.

## The Things People Always Regret Bringing

A nice camera they're afraid to use. Dress shoes. A full-size towel (guesthouses have them; get a microfibre if yours don't). More than two books (download them; physical books are heavy and you can swap them at traveller hostels anyway).

## Final Word

When in doubt, leave it out. The best souvenir from Southeast Asia is a lighter bag on the way home because you bought better things than you brought.
