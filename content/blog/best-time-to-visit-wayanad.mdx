## The Conventional Answer

Every travel blog will tell you the same thing: visit Wayanad between **October and May**. The skies are clear, the trekking trails are dry, and the waterfalls are flowing (but not dangerously so). This advice is correct, as far as it goes.

But there's more to the story.

## The Case for the Monsoon (June–September)

Here's what those guides don't mention: Wayanad during the monsoon is something else entirely.

The mist hangs so low it brushes the ground. The tea estates turn an almost fluorescent green. The waterfalls are thundering, the leeches are out (bring salt), and the crowds are almost entirely absent.

If you're a photographer, a writer, or someone who doesn't mind getting wet — the monsoon version of Wayanad is one of the most atmospheric experiences in India.

**What to know before going in monsoon:**
- The Chembra Peak trek is closed (too dangerous)
- Roads can flood — always check conditions before driving
- Book a plantation stay, not a hilltop resort — you want to be in the forest, not above it
- Pack waterproof bags for your camera gear

## October–November: The Sweet Spot

If you want the best of everything — moderate crowds, green landscapes still recovering from the rains, flowing waterfalls, and open trekking trails — the **post-monsoon window of October to November** is unbeatable.

The air is clean, the roads have dried out, and the whole district smells of cardamom.

## December–February: Peak Season (and Why That's Fine)

Yes, it's busier. Yes, Chembra Peak will have queues on weekends. But the weather is perfect, accommodation prices are high but justified, and this is when you're most likely to find festivals and cultural events happening in the villages.

Book at least two weeks ahead for any decent homestay.

## March–May: Hot But Uncrowded

By March, the temperature climbs and the landscape starts to look dusty at the edges. But this is also when the summer fruit season starts — jackfruit, mango, and woodapple. The waterfalls are thinner but still beautiful, and you'll often have the Edakkal Caves almost to yourself.

## The Bottom Line

There is no bad time to visit Wayanad — only different versions of it. Decide what kind of experience you want, and go accordingly.

We've done Wayanad in every season. Our personal favourite? Early November, just after the rains. Every time.
