## Overview

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Wayanad is Kerala's best-kept secret — a highland district where dense coffee estates meet ancient tribal villages, and where every morning begins in a sea of mist. This itinerary skips the tourist hotspots in favour of trails and experiences that feel genuinely undiscovered.

## Day 1 — Arrival & Kalpetta Exploration

Arrive at Calicut Airport and transfer to Wayanad (approximately 3 hours). Check into your eco-stay in the hills above Kalpetta. In the evening, take a leisurely walk through Kalpetta's market and old town, picking up local spices and chatting with the vendors who've been there for generations.

**Stay:** Kalpetta Heritage Homestay

## Day 2 — Chembra Peak Trek

An early 5am start for the Chembra Peak trail — the highest peak in Wayanad at 2,100m. The route passes through grasslands and shola forests before opening up to a breathtaking heart-shaped lake near the summit. Return to the homestay for a late lunch and a slow afternoon.

**Distance:** 12km round trip | **Duration:** ~7 hours

## Day 3 — Edakkal Caves & Ambalavayal

After breakfast, drive to the Edakkal Caves — a system of natural caverns with Neolithic carvings dating back over 5,000 years. In the afternoon, visit the Wayanad Heritage Museum in Ambalavayal to understand the indigenous Adivasi communities of the region.

## Day 4 — Pookode Lake & Plantation Walk

Morning kayaking on the serene Pookode Lake. After lunch, a guided walk through a working spice plantation where you'll learn to identify cardamom, pepper, vanilla, and nutmeg in their natural state. End the day at a viewpoint overlooking the valleys below.

## Day 5 — Banasura Sagar & Departure

A morning boat ride on Banasura Sagar, the largest earthen dam in India, before transferring back to Calicut Airport for your flight home.
