## Overview

Kerala's backwaters are one of the world's most iconic waterway networks — a labyrinth of canals, lagoons, and lakes stretching along the Arabian Sea coast. This itinerary pairs a private houseboat journey with the cultural richness of Fort Kochi and the quiet peace of Marari Beach.

## Day 1 — Arrive Kochi

Arrive at Cochin International Airport. Transfer to your boutique guesthouse in Fort Kochi. Evening stroll along the waterfront — the iconic Chinese fishing nets are especially photogenic at sunset.

## Day 2 — Fort Kochi Deep Dive

A full day with a local heritage guide exploring Fort Kochi's layers: the Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue, St. Francis Church (the oldest European church in India), and the vibrant street art of Princess Street. Evening Kathakali performance at a traditional theatre.

## Day 3 — Kochi to Alleppey

Drive south to Alleppey and board your private houseboat. Spend the afternoon drifting through narrow canals as cormorants dive alongside and village life unfolds on the banks. Dinner is prepared fresh on board by your personal cook.

## Day 4 — Backwaters at Dawn

Early morning mist on the water, coffee on deck, and the sound of oars. Explore smaller side canals on a country boat before returning to the main houseboat for a late breakfast. Disembark at Alleppey in the afternoon.

## Day 5 — Marari Beach

Transfer to Marari, a quiet fishing village beach 10km north of Alleppey. Spend the day at leisure — cycling through coconut groves, watching the fishermen sort their catch, or simply reading in a hammock.

## Day 6 — Coir Village & Cooking Class

Visit a traditional coir-making village to see how coconut husks are transformed into rope and matting. Afternoon cooking class with a local home cook — learn to make fish curry, appam, and payasam.

## Day 7 — Departure

Morning at leisure before transfer to Cochin Airport.
