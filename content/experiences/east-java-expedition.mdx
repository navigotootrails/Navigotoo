## What Awaits You

East Java is Indonesia's most dramatic volcanic landscape. This expedition takes you into the crater of Kawah Ijen — where sulphur miners work by night and electric-blue fire glows in the pre-dawn darkness — and then to Mount Bromo's caldera for one of the world's most iconic sunrises.

## The Ijen Blue Fire Experience

The blue fire at Ijen is one of nature's most extraordinary phenomena — electric-blue flames caused by combusting sulphuric gases, only visible in darkness. You'll start the trek at 1am, descend into the active crater with a guide, and witness the glow before sunrise floods the caldera with gold.

**Note:** This requires a moderate level of fitness. Gas masks are provided as the sulphur fumes can be intense.

## Mount Bromo at Sunrise

On day three, a 4WD jeep transfers you through the Sea of Sand to the viewpoint above Bromo's caldera. Watching the sun rise over a panorama of volcanic peaks is a moment that stays with you long after you've returned home.

## What to Expect

This is not a gentle tour. The Ijen trek involves a 3km descent into an active volcanic crater on a rough path at night. The Bromo sunrise involves a very early start (around 3am). Both are entirely worth it.

Participants should be comfortable with moderate hiking and have no serious respiratory conditions.
